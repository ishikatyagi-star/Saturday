Newton's First Law (Inertia)
An object at rest stays at rest unless acted upon by an unbalanced force. This is called the law of inertia.
Objects resist changes in motion
More mass means more inertia
Net force of zero means constant velocity
💡 Like pushing a shopping cart — a full cart is harder to start and harder to stop.

Chatbot Screen (Feature 4)
What is Newton's First Law?
Newton's First Law, also called the Law of Inertia, states that an object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.
Give me an example
Sure! When a car suddenly brakes, your body lurches forward — that's inertia. Your body wants to keep moving forward even though the car is stopping. That's why we wear seatbelts — they apply an external force to stop your body's motion.
